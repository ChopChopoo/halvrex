Frequently asked questions

Common questions about Halvrex shoes and ordering.

Will the Halvrex Guide work for severe overpronation?

The Guide is designed for mild to moderate overpronation. Runners with severe pronation may need a more aggressive stability shoe.

How does the SymFlow differ from a traditional medial post?

A medial post is a hard plastic insert. The SymFlow is a density gradient in the midsole foam itself. It activates only when the foot rolls inward.

How many miles before I should replace the Guide?

We design the Guide for 400 to 500 miles. The midsole keeps its stability characteristics through that range.

Can I use the Guide for racing?

The Guide is a daily trainer, not a race shoe. For race-day we recommend the Light if you still want stability, or a non-stability racer if you only pronate at slow paces.

What is the difference between the Guide and the Guide LR?

The LR has a 2 mm higher stack and a slightly softer foam. It is built for long efforts where the extra cushioning helps.

Are Halvrex shoes available in wide widths?

The Guide and Guide LR are available in standard and wide widths. The Light is currently standard width only.
